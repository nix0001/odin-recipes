Menudo

Description

Menudo refers to two completely different, distinct dishes depending on the region. In Mexican cuisine, it is a traditional spicy soup made with beef tripe and hominy in a rich red-chili broth. In Filipino cuisine, it is a hearty, tomato-based stew typically prepared with cubed pork, liver, carrots, potatoes, and hotdogs.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef tripe
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 4 (15 ounce) cans white hominy
  • ¼ teaspoon chili powder
  • salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. In a 16 quart pot combine the tripe and the onions. Add water until pot is about 3/4 full.
  2. Cover and cook on low heat for about 2 hours, or until the tripe is tender
  3. Add the hominy, chili powder, and salt and pepper to taste
  4. Cover and cook for another 45 minutes to one hour to incorporate the flavors
  5. Serve with fresh onions, corn tortillas and lemon if desired.
